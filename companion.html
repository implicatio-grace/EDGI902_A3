The architecture of the argument
Definitional canopy → readings → themes → design principles

The A3 evidence network after the CLT, heutagogy + temporal batches: 59 papers (26 A2-baseline + 33 A3; 3 reserve excluded) across four layers. A function-graded definitional canopy (top) supplies constructs, frameworks, context and method — now with a Temporal / social-time group warranting the learner-owned temporal register (design-led, evidence-convergent); gap-framing papers (left) open the argumentative space; readings cluster by source literature into cross-cutting themes that justify four design principles (DPs). Edge weight encodes evidential directness; tier dots encode journal standing; ⚠ markers surface productive tensions and citation-confidence flags. The Conversations filter traces eleven cross-paper exchanges and the designer-to-learner causal chain.

How to read it
Fifty-nine papers in four layers. The function-graded definitional canopy (top) supplies constructs (Conklin, Knowles, Sweller, Kalyuga 2003, the Hase & Kenyon heutagogy trio, Argyris), frameworks (Arthur & Passini, Raubal & Egenhofer, Blaschke, Glassner & Back Ch6), context (Samuel) and method (van den Akker, Narayan) — function badges, not tier dots; faint dotted lines drop into the DPs each anchors. Gap-framing papers (left) name the argumentative space the DP arc fills; Agonács adds a rationale-level heutagogy-evidence gap. Readings cluster by source literature — eight clusters now: the Cognitive load (CLT) group in its own colour, Assessment & feedback (Carless), a new Temporal / social-time canopy group (most of the stream is beyond A3 scope; only the register's warrant is lit), and Heutagogy split from SDL (toggle). Tier dots and badges encode standing; edge weight encodes how directly each link is licensed: mechanistic (the DP2 core — Miller, Krieglstein, Amadieu), defensible (empirical in design-relevant contexts), interpretive (Kim's macro-warrant; the construct canopy), consistent-with (reviews, the heutagogy convergent set, course readings).
What it surfaces
Sources & caveats
Tier scheme: T1 flagship → T4 limited, anchored on field-relative IF and venue type. The scheme grades journal standing, not study quality — Bhatti (T4, real conceptual load) and DeStefano (T1 venue, review) show the two are not equivalent. Definitional papers are graded by architectural function, not tier. The Hase & Kenyon heutagogy trio renders as one grouped unit with a cite-divide (Ch1 = how enacted; Ch2 = why + axis theory + continuum caveat; 2001 = axis origin) to avoid triple-counting; the Alhadeff-Jones cluster likewise renders as one grouped node with a cite-divide (2017 monograph = citation of record; the 2019/2020/2023/2010 facets = specific-point cites), carrying the single-scholar caveat. Sorokin & Merton is the highest-cited node (~1,409 GS) yet the most out-of-scope (1937 macro-sociology) — tier ≠ load. Shahjahan & Bano's weight rests on in-context relevance (Adult Learning; WoS ESCI, no JCR JIF), not impact factor. Two cells are open KM decisions — Agonács tier and Glassner & Back Ch5 §7.1 — rendered with current values and flagged (⚑). Citations: Google Scholar, May 2026. Conversations are labelled exchanges from the synthesis, not new edges. Tay, Schwarz and Goldstein are reserve — excluded as nodes.
